# Relay — Pitch / Positioning One-Pager

**Tagline:** The ambient co-pilot that answers from *your* knowledge — live, mid-conversation.

---

## Problem

Customer-facing teams live on calls and drown in documents. Sellers spend only ~25% of their time selling; ~64% goes to hunting for information. When a question lands on a live call, the answer exists in a doc somewhere — but finding it takes 30 seconds the conversation doesn't have. Today's live-assist tools generate plausible answers from a general model with no access to your real knowledge: confident, and often wrong.

## Solution

Relay listens to a live conversation and, the instant a question or topic comes up, retrieves the answer from your own documents — sub-10 ms via Moss — and renders a grounded, **cited** card on screen in under half a second. Not a chatbot. Grounded retrieval, in real time.

## Why now

- Voice models got cheap and fast — retrieval became the bottleneck, and sub-10 ms retrieval (Moss) just made real-time grounding possible.
- The viral live-assist tools proved demand but have no company-knowledge grounding — exactly the gap we fill.

## Product — one engine, three agents

- **Relay Live** (Co-Pilot): surfaces context on live calls.
- **Relay Desk** (Support): resolves with docs + customer history.
- **Relay Intake** (Lead Gen): qualifies and routes inbound leads.

## Market

Customer-facing teams at 10–50 person companies — an underserved segment between DIY tools and six-figure enterprise platforms. Adjacent categories (knowledge management, sales enablement, AI support) are each multi-billion-dollar and validated by breakout players; none is voice-native and grounded in your knowledge in real time.

## Moat

Real-time retrieval at conversation latency is genuinely hard, and grounding-by-citation builds the trust generic assistants can't. The more a team uses Relay, the better its knowledge base and memory get — a compounding data advantage.

## Traction plan

Landing page + waitlist live within 24h of the hackathon; target ≥20 qualified signups before the YC interview. First design partners from the SMB teams who feel the pain most acutely.

## The ask

Feedback, design partners, and a conversation about what "replacing the scramble for information" looks like at scale.
